OEM Manufacturing Process Optimization

Separated raw material costs before production by redesigning the manufacturing process with co-products, enabling more accurate inventory valuation and product costing.

Business areaProduction & costing
My roleProcess and costing design
Core conceptCo-products
OutcomeDetailed cost visibility

01 · Business Challenge

The same raw material cost was used across different product grades.

Different finished-goods grades consumed materials of different quality, but the original process allocated the same average raw material cost across production jobs.

Manufacturing Process Design · Before & AfterClick the diagram to enlarge
The diagram compares the original manufacturing and costing flow with the redesigned co-product process.

02 · Business Effects

Broad cost allocation reduced product-cost accuracy.

Premium products undervalued

Higher-quality material consumption was not fully reflected in product cost.

Standard products overvalued

Lower-quality material inherited part of the premium material cost.

Inventory valuation mismatch

Material groups did not carry independent, business-relevant values.

Profitability analysis weakened

Selling price and unit-cost comparisons were less representative.

04 · Solution Design

Separate material groups before the main production process.

An intermediate material-preparation job produced dedicated material groups as co-products. Each group received its own cost allocation before being issued to the corresponding production job.

PrepareIssue the original raw material into a preparation job.
Separate & AllocateCreate co-products and distribute quantity and cost by business rules.
Consume CorrectlyProduction jobs consume the appropriate material group and cost.

05 · Business Impact

More accurate valuation and flexible OEM costing.

Improved inventory valuation accuracy.
Enabled product costing by material quality.
Increased flexibility for OEM manufacturing requirements.
Reduced reliance on broad average-cost allocation.
