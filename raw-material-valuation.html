Raw Material Valuation & Cost Allocation

A functional solution for fresh raw material receiving across multiple stations, covering quality inspection, controlled pricing, transportation-related valuation, and ERP receipt creation.

Scale5+ receiving stations
Volume600+ tons per month
Transactions15,000+ receipts supported
My roleFunctional design & coordination

01 · Business Challenge

Manual steps created pricing, receipt, and valuation risks.

Paper-based quality inspection

Inspection results and quality calculations were recorded manually, increasing errors and receiving rework.

Manual pricing and receipt entry

Quality grading, price adjustments, and ERP receipts depended on manual calculations and data entry.

Transportation weight differences

Factory receipt quantity could differ from the station quantity, while supplier payment value had to remain unchanged.

Month-end accounting adjustments

Transportation cost was paid separately and manually journaled into raw material cost before closing.

03 · Solution Overview

Move control and valuation logic into the receiving process.

Controlled pricing master data

Pricing rules, grading criteria, promotions, and adjustments were centralized and activated through approval.

Digital quality inspection

Tablet-based inspection added validation and automatic quality calculations in place of paper forms.

Automated receiving calculation

Weighing and QA data were combined to calculate grade quantities, price adjustments, transportation cost, and final supplier value.

Factory-side revaluation

Final received quantity was used to recalculate unit cost while preserving the total supplier payment value.
